Glass Fold -- Privacy Policy

Last updated: 22 June 2026

Glass Fold is a single-player puzzle game with an optional community level-sharing feature. We keep data collection to the minimum needed to run the game. We do not sell your data, and the game does not require an account, name, or email to play.

What stays on your device

Your progress, stars, coins, daily streak, unlocked themes, and settings (sound, haptics, theme, and so on) are stored locally on your device. They are not uploaded to us and are removed if you delete the app.

Community levels (optional)

If you publish a level, vote on one, or report one, that action is sent to our backend (Google Firebase / Firestore) so others can see it. To do this the app creates an anonymous sign-in -- a random device identifier with no name, email, or contact information attached. What we store for this feature:

  • The level you published (its title and layout) and the publish time.
  • Your up/down votes and any reports, tied only to the anonymous id (to prevent duplicate voting and abuse).

If you never use the community feature, none of this leaves your device. To have a published level or your community data removed, contact us at the address below.

Advertising

The game may show ads through Google AdMob (for example, an optional "watch an ad for more folds"). AdMob may collect device identifiers and similar information to serve and measure ads. On iOS you will be asked for permission (App Tracking Transparency) before any tracking identifier is used; you can decline and still play. See Google's Privacy Policy for details. Removing ads via a purchase stops ad requests.

Purchases

In-app purchases (such as removing ads) are processed by the Apple App Store and managed through RevenueCat. We do not receive or store your payment card details. We only receive confirmation of whether a purchase is active so we can apply it.

Children

Glass Fold is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from them. If you believe a child has provided information through the community feature, contact us and we will remove it.

Your choices

  • Delete the app to remove all locally stored data.
  • Decline the iOS tracking prompt to limit ad personalisation.
  • Contact us to remove community content tied to your device.
